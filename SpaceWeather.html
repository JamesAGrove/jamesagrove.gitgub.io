Space Weather

Space weather forecasting does not predict personality types but does forecast threats from space that threaten life and the services we rely on to live our modern lives. In 2011 the UK Government placed weather from space on its National Risk Register of Civil Emergencies (Met Office, 2024).

What causes space weather?

Factors that influence space weather include: geomagnetism; solar activity; atmosphere; and the Earth’s position in the solar system.

The most impactful hazard from space par a major asteroid collision are coronal mass ejections & solar flares. The release of large electromagnetic energy from the Sun can arrive at the Earth’s atmosphere between 8.5 minutes and multiple days (Met Office, 2024). The Earth’s atmosphere absorbs the electromagnetic radiation before it reaches the Earth’s surface. Interaction between radiation, magnetic fields and particles from the Sun with those in Earth’s atmosphere have impact in the ionosphere. This part of Earth’s atmosphere refracts radio communication waves over long distances but during space weather events the ionosphere is ionised resulting in radio waves experiencing more frequent collisions that cause radio waves to lose energy and the ability to be received (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, 2024).

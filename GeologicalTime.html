Geological Time

Whilst studying geological time is focused on the events of the past, the events that shaped the geology of the Earth today can aid identifying areas that pose hazard to human life. Additionally, the frequency and force of ongoing/present geological processes is an important factor determining risks and will be covered in later sections (see anthropogenic hazards).

The United Kingdom has not been forever United, England and Scotland were formerly not adjoined. The Grampian and Caledonian Orogeny saw Scotland collide with England and Wales as the lapteus ocean closed during the end of the Silurian period forming the Highlands that were created in a similar manner and to a similar scale to the Himalayan Mountains (Toghill, 2020).

Conclusion

The hazards posed by geological processes past and present are wide ranging in the United Kingdom. Investment in understanding and forecasting risks is considerable in order to match the potential damage to property and life. Threats to modern living stem not only from the ground and visible material but also from particles and radiation from space that interact with the magnetism and atmospheric protection that surrounds Earth. Advances in forecasting can help early warning of the public to potential threats but some hazards, particularly landslides, are harder to predict. Ground investigation is integral to mitigating risks not only because they inform models such as flood models but also the integrity of human infrastructure such as dams and buildings. Overall the geological history that formed a locations common substrate alters regional risk.

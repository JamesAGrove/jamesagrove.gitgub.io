Landslides

A landslide is the downslope movement of loose soil and/or rock that occurs when the force of gravity acting on a slope exceeds the resisting forces of that slope (British Geological Survey, 2024). Multiple factors influence landslides occurrence, including: landscape morphology; rock composition; and the stress on the substrate above and below ground (see below for more).

Landslide causes

In the case of a local selected case study, Luddenden Foot landslip (see below), frost shattering where water seeps into the cracks of rock and expands as it freezes (Park, 2018), may have been a contributing factor as the incident occurred after a period of sub zero temperatures. Furthermore, it is possible that weathering caused by the adjacent stream influenced the slip.
